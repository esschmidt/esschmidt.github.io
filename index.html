ABOUT

Photo by Gilbert Santana

I'm an MLIS student at the Pratt Institute School of Information (expected graduation May 2021) with particular interests in reference and outreach, information-seeking behavior and needs, and technical services. I am currently a fellow at The Met's Thomas J. Watson Library focused on digital collections and systems projects. I also work in the Access Services department of the Newman Library at Baruch College, CUNY. My volunteer experience includes two years as a citizenship exam prep coach at a public library and in sales and inventory at a non-profit bookstore.

Before moving into librarianship, I spent eight years working at non-profit organizations focused on architecture, design, and the built environment. As a program manager focused on housing issues at The Architectural League of New York, I organized numerous events (including a yearlong discussion series and a daylong conference) on pressing issues at the intersection of design, policy, and politics in housing. I also regularly wrote, commissioned articles, conducted interviews, and edited for the organization's website and its magazine, Urban Omnibus. Previously, as a project manager at the community design center bcWORKSHOP, I worked on community storytelling, neighborhood mapping, and data analysis. I hold a B.A. in American studies, with a concentration in urban studies, from Wesleyan University.

GRADUATE STUDENT WORK

View or download (as PDFs) a sample of my projects completed at Pratt Institute:

Reconsidering Intellectual Freedom in the Library

A research paper exploring the limitations of and harms caused by the prevailing interpretations of intellectual freedom in libraries, which privilege a right to speech over values of safety and participation (Fall 2019, Foundations of Information, Prof. Lopatovska).

Critical Ed-Tech

A hand-coded website, created with two Pratt classmates, which explores the prevalence of and problems with the use of educational technology in K-12 schools (Fall 2019, Information Technologies, Prof. Maceli).

Metadata Application Profile for ETDs

A metadata application profile—including domain model, cataloging guidelines, and element set—for a collection of electronic theses and dissertations (ETDs), created with two Pratt classmates (Fall 2020, Metadata Design, Prof. Provo).

Time Traveling with Historical Maps

A lesson plan and activities for an hourlong online educational experience to teach middle and high school students about maps as historical documents and map literacy (Spring 2020, Museum & Library Outreach, Prof. Baumann).

Collective Action for Digital Accessibility

A research paper examining accessibility issues with electronic resources in academic libraries, arguing for the need for collective action a professional ethic to improve accessibility (Fall 2020, Collection Development, Prof. Ballmer).

Reference Review: Dictionary of American Regional English (DARE)

An evaluation of the DARE electronic database, reviewing its features, uses, and strengths and shortcomings (Fall 2019, Reference & Instruction, Prof. Smith-Cruz).
