I'm Emily (she/her), a new graduate of the MSLIS program at the Pratt Institute School of Information. I currently work in the Access Services department of the Newman Library at Baruch College, CUNY, and was recently a fellow at The Metropolitan Museum of Art's Watson Library, where I supported digital collections and systems projects that highlight and improve access to the library's resources.

Before moving into librarianship, I spent eight years at architecture and design non-profit organizations. As a program manager at The Architectural League of New York, I organized numerous events (including a yearlong discussion series and a daylong conference) and wrote and edited articles on issues of design, policy, and politics in housing. Previously, as a project manager at the community design center bcWORKSHOP, I worked on community storytelling, neighborhood mapping, and data analysis projects.

I'm originally from Chicago and I hold a BA in American studies from Wesleyan University.

GRADUATE STUDENT WORK

My coursework at Pratt included Art Librarianship, Collection Development, Community Building & Engagement, Digital Preservation & Curation, Foundations of Information, Information Technologies, Knowledge Organization, Management of Archives & Special Collections, Metadata Design, Museum & Library Outreach, Programming for Cultural Heritage, and Reference & Instruction.

View or download a sample of my projects:

Reconsidering Intellectual Freedom in the Library (PDF)

A research paper exploring the limitations of and harms caused by the prevailing interpretations of intellectual freedom in libraries, which privilege a right to speech over values of safety and participation (Fall 2019, Foundations of Information, Prof. Lopatovska).

Critical Ed-Tech

A hand-coded website, created with two Pratt classmates, which explores the prevalence of and problems with the use of educational technology in K-12 schools (Fall 2019, Information Technologies, Prof. Maceli).

Metadata Application Profile for ETDs (PDF)

A metadata application profile—including domain model, cataloging guidelines, and element set—for a collection of electronic theses and dissertations (ETDs), created with two Pratt classmates (Fall 2020, Metadata Design, Prof. Provo).

Time Traveling with Historical Maps (PDF)

A lesson plan and activities for an hourlong online educational experience to teach middle and high school students about maps as historical documents and map literacy (Spring 2020, Museum & Library Outreach, Prof. Baumann).

Collective Action for Digital Accessibility (PDF)

A research paper examining accessibility issues with electronic resources in academic libraries, arguing for the need for collective action and a professional ethic to improve accessibility (Fall 2020, Collection Development, Prof. Ballmer).

Review: Dictionary of American Regional English (PDF)

An evaluation of the Dictionary of American Regional English electronic database, reviewing its features, uses, and strengths and shortcomings (Fall 2019, Reference & Instruction, Prof. Smith-Cruz).
